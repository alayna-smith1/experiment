What is something you're passionate about?

I am passionate about maximizing my health, specifically using food as a means of nourishment. I love listening to books and podcasts (usually at the gym/on a run) on physiology and nutrient metabolism, particularly for high performance athletes. My current personal favorites are "The Proof" by Simon Hill, and "Fiber Fueled".

What is something you're proud of?

I am proud of my consistency with my fitness and health goals. For the past 9 weeks (Oct 15 - Dec 15), I have maintatined a streak of hitting my macronutrient goals nearly every single day. In addition, I have built a rock-solid habit of daily exercise (sometimes two sessions, to decompress in the evenings) over the past 18 months. From a nutritional standpoint, I aim to include as many whole foods as possible, focusing on nutrient density and variety.

What is something you're not proud of? How has that shaped you?

For years, I have struggled to be punctual to meetings and in-person commitments. Homework and project deadlines? Almost never late. But 9am church meetings, and I find myself leaving the house at 9am. On my mission, I learned by necessity to plan ahead and leave room for error. I am much-improved in punctuality comparatively, however, this is still an area that I can improve on. I want to model respect in word and deed, particularly how I treat others' time, whether I get in trouble or not.
