Mission Statement

My mission is to joyfully magnify my stewardships by living with loving intentionality — strengthening my relationship with God, uplifting those I serve, and empowering others to draw closer to Him. As a wife and mother, I will create faith-centered places that invite peace and acceptance. As a professional, I will lead with integrity, diligence, and excellence. As a master gatherer, I lead with love, ambition, and purpose; I work diligently to develop my body, mind, and spirit, that I may be a light to others, helping them find their worth, hope, and divine potential.

Core Values

Integrity

When I know something is right, I will do it. While serving a mission for the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, I practiced this mantra daily. Early on, I learned that is applies, whether I feel like doing the thing or not. On one sunny day, while enjoying some of the little free-time I had during the week, (a time where I generally relaxed and chose not engage with others) I felt a strong impression to approach a woman sitting on a bench next to me. At first, I resented the idea, as I was in the middle of my lunch - carrot in hand - and more significantly, since I wasn't "required" to do any missionary work at that time. However, the question, "what sign would you like to give to the Lord?" popped in my mind, and I realized that above all else, how I act is always noted by God. With this in mind, I got up, started a conversation with the woman, and made a connection that grew for another 6+ months; our friendship stood out above all other interactions on my mission and built my testimony of God's love for each of His children. I never want to give up an opportunity to do the right thing, simply because it is inconvenient.

Growth

A leader in my life growing up once told me, "I will never not do something just because I am scared." I took that personal rule to heart and ran with it. Because of it, I chose to volunteer as my high school's concert choir accompanist, even though nothing scared me more than sight-reading and playing in that setting. Because of that choice, I decided to go out and sell door-to-door as a 21-year old, just 5 months off of my mission, with no sales experience, or any friends on the team. Because of it, I chose to show up nearly every week to iHub Networking and Treehouse Events (events catered to entrepreurs) without any social connections or personal business to relate to others there. That choice also led me to run my first half-marathon, which led to a marathon a few months ago, and a triathlon that I will complete in March 2026. These experiences have deeply shaped who I am today, and continue to guide me in my everyday and long-term decision-making.

Consistency

I believe in the law of compounding. Small, consistent actions over time lead to remarkable results. I first learned this as a pianist, having studied the instrument since age 6, eventually taking that passion through my first year of college on-scholarship. Daily drills on the same boring scales, arpeggios, and passages seemed repetitive and unhelpful for years. However, especially as I dialed-in my tehcnique in college, I rapidly witnessed the reward of those drills in my performance and ability to quickly apply my professor's feedback. Now, I am prepared to see no results for things that matter to me for a long time, as I trust the process. What takes muscles months to build, may show up suddenly in performance, and builds consistnecy and reliability. I focus on "watering the grass in front of me," choosing to focus on what I have versus what I haven't. As Grant Cardone - one of my inspirations - says, "Everything worth doing is worth doing everyday."

Food is love, and I have seen it build bridges of understanding and compassion that surpass communication. In high school, I spent cooked nearly every night for my family (as my mom hated cooking). I then spent sunday afternon bakinga and delivering treat to neighbors I did not know. The same on my mission. Most meaningful as been the friendships I've built over gatherings in college.
